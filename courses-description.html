ENGLISH

Short-Story/Creative Writing Practice: Flexible Schedule & Different Formats available; Individual & Small Group Courses for Young Adults & Adults (Packages of 10 & 20 hour sessions; Written feedback on all submissions)
Instructor: Troy Fillman
“The trick is to turn your own life into something that has meaning for people whose experience is nothing like your own.”— Jeanette Winterson
Our lives are full of meaning and significance, yet many fail to recognize the story hidden within their lives. In this course, student are encouraged to read and write short stories as part of a daily/weekly reflective practice. Short story writing enhances a broad range of language & literacy skills at any age.
This course promotes personal reflection, creative thinking, audience awareness, self-expression, vocabulary development, and personal growth.
Explore your world and perspectives by creating stories with deeper meaning for you and others. Prerequisites: a love of language and writing; a genuine willingness to explore what is meaningful to you; advanced English language fluency.
Academic Writing Practice Focus: Writing a Reflective Essay-Intensive Course 10 hours/ 2 Weeks, Flexible Schedule, Individual & Small Group Courses (4 to 8 weeks) are available
Instructor: Troy Fillman
In this short course, students practice examining his or her experience for meaning, personal growth, closure, and more.
Student are also encouraged to view “periodic and daily reflection” as an essential life practice that can help lead to personal, academic, and success.
All drafts will receive written feedback and suggestions for improvement. Students who complete the assignment successfully may opt to submit/use it for school assignments and/or satisfy application requirements for college & university programs. Peer editing may be required in small-group classes.
